\section{Conclusion}

WiFi-TTA-Bench shows TTA outcomes on WiFi CSI depend jointly on shift severity, backbone, and method: no primary-MLP method clears a positive 95\% CI on any real dataset, yet faithful BN-only TENT on MLP+BN reaches $+$21.1~pp on SignFi while staying near zero on Widar's hard shift, and faithful SAR/CoTTA shrink toward zero (Table~\ref{tab:lame_sar_cotta_cross})---so simplified ports and BatchNorm alone do not explain the failure. Three mechanisms are consistent with the evidence---overconfidence (Table~\ref{tab:overconfidence}), batch-statistic unreliability, and a synthetic-to-real physics-prior swing---and source-side label smoothing reduces the negative-adaptation rate $0.47\!\to\!0.27$ (Appendix~\ref{app:source_overfit}). All splits, faithful methods, and Croissant 1.0 metadata are publicly released.
